05 — DECISIONS

EVERY CHOICE HAD A REASON.

WHY DUAL MODE?

User research showed comfort levels vary widely. A standing-only mode removes the barrier for beginners while the full yoga flow serves experienced practitioners. Accessibility designed from day one.

WHY NEON ON BLACK?

Fluorescent on black creates a visual metaphor for energy made visible. Magenta and fuchsia carry spiritual, transformative associations. The dark background makes the user's aura the most important thing in the space.

WHY SEGMENTATION?

Rigged 3D models proved unreliable. MediaPipe's built-in segmentation generates the user's actual silhouette — more accurate, more personal, more immediate. The user sees themselves as energy, not a generic avatar.

WHY MEDIAPIPE?

Real-time body tracking without additional hardware. Works on any standard webcam. 33 landmarks at 60fps. Actively maintained by Google. No setup required for exhibition visitors.

07 — CHALLENGES

PROBLEMS THAT MADE THE PROJECT STRONGER.

CHALLENGE

Webcam has no depth perception. How to create a 3D-feeling body visualization from a 2D source?

SOLUTION

MediaPipe segmentation generates the user's actual silhouette. Glow passes and gradient fill create perceived depth without real 3D data.

CHALLENGE

Rigged 3D models from Sketchfab weren't properly rigged. No bones, wouldn't load in Three.js.

SOLUTION

Pivoted to segmentation mask. More unique, better performing, more personal — users see their own shape, not a generic avatar.

CHALLENGE

Running MediaPipe + Three.js + p5.js simultaneously caused performance and interference issues.

SOLUTION

Separate canvas layers with independent animation loops. Each system isolated. 60fps maintained across all three.
